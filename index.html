Methodology Overview

Netzerra implements IPCC 2006 Guidelines updated with AR6 (2021) GWP₁₀₀ values. Calculations follow the GHG Protocol Scope 1/2/3 framework aligned with ISO 14064-1:2018.

⚡ Kenya Grid EF — Verified March 2026: Kenya's grid is ~90% renewable (geothermal 44%, hydro 23%, wind 16%). Correct EF: 0.070 kgCO₂e/kWh (IEA 2024 / SEforAll Kenya). A figure of 0.497 kgCO₂e/kWh was previously incorrect and has been corrected across all Scope 2 calculations.
Emissions (tCO₂e) = Σ [Activity Data × Emission Factor × GWP₁₀₀] ÷ 1000
AR6 GWP₁₀₀(CH₄) = 27.0, GWP₁₀₀(N₂O) = 273 — GHG Protocol Aug 2024 / IPCC AR6 WG1 Ch.7
AR5 GWP₁₀₀(CH₄) = 28, GWP₁₀₀(N₂O) = 265 — UNFCCC Paris Agreement standard

Borehole Methodology

Scope 1: diesel (drilling + pumping + generator). Scope 2: KPLC grid × (1 − solar%). Scope 3: casing materials amortised over design lifetime.

S1 = (L_drill/lifetime + L_pump + L_gen) × 2.68
S2 = kWh × 0.070 × (1 − solar%) [Kenya grid, IEA 2024]
S3 = (kg_steel×1850 + kg_PVC×2410 + kg_cement×830) / lifetime / 1000
Total [tCO₂e/yr] = (S1 + S2 + S3) / 1000

Livestock Methodology

IPCC 2006 Vol.4 Ch.10 Tier 1 — Eastern Africa defaults. Enteric CH₄ + manure CH₄ + pasture N₂O. GWP values are dynamically applied from the active GWP set (AR6 default).

E_enteric = Σ(heads × EF_ent) × GWP_CH4 [AR6: 27.0 | AR5: 28]
E_manure_CH4 = Σ(heads × EF_man_CH4) × GWP_CH4
E_manure_N2O = Σ(heads × Nex × EF3 × 44/28) × GWP_N2O [AR6: 273 | AR5: 265]
EF3 = 0.02 pasture (IPCC 2006 Vol.4 Table 11.1)
Total [tCO₂e/yr] = (E_ent + E_man + E_n2o + E_feed + E_elec) / 1000
📋 Uncertainty: ±30% enteric CH₄, ±50% manure N₂O (IPCC 2006 Vol.4 Table 1.4). Tier 1 values. For Tier 2 accuracy, use country-specific EFs from direct measurement.

Transport Methodology

DEFRA/BEIS 2023 fuel-based method. Refrigerant fugitives classified as Scope 3 — use active GWP set.

E_fuel = Σ(L_fuel × EF_fuel) / 1000 [diesel 2.68, petrol 2.31 — IPCC 2006 Vol.2]
E_HFC = kg_HFC134a × GWP_HFC134a [AR6: 1,530 | AR5: 1,300 — GHG Protocol Aug 2024]
E_R404 = kg_R404A × GWP_R404A [AR6: 4,180 | AR5: 3,922]
Total [tCO₂e/yr] = (E_fuel + E_HFC + E_R404) / 1000
⚠️ Note: HFC-134a GWP changed significantly between assessment reports. AR4=1,430, AR5=1,300, AR6=1,530. The GWP selector above controls which value is applied.

Construction Methodology

Scope 3 embodied carbon (Bath ICE v3.0 cradle-to-gate) typically dominates construction projects. Leakage from land clearing is added to the total if a land type is selected.

Scope 3 = t_cement×830 + m³_conc×159 + t_steel×1850 + t_rebar×1990 + ... [Bath ICE v3.0]
Scope 1 = (L_excav + L_gen) × months × 2.68 + truck_km × 0.20 [IPCC 2006 / DEFRA 2023]
Scope 2 = kWh/mo × months × 0.070 [IEA 2024 — Kenya grid]
Leakage = hectares × tCO₂e/ha [IPCC 2006 Vol.4 Table 4.7 — Eastern Africa land cover]
Total = Scope1 + Scope2 + Scope3 + Leakage
📋 Uncertainty: ±20% steel, ±15% concrete, ±10% cement (Bath ICE v3.0 standard deviations). For KNCR Level 2 accuracy, use contractor-verified material quantities.

Manufacturing Methodology

IPCC 2006 Vol.3. Emission intensity benchmarked against KTDA, IFC, FAO, World Steel sector references. CBAM-ready output for EU export compliance.

Scope 1 = L_diesel×2.68 + L_HFO×3.17 + kg_LPG×2.98 + t_CO2×1000 [IPCC 2006 Vol.2]
Scope 2 = (kWh_grid − kWh_solar) × 0.070 [IEA 2024 — Kenya grid]
Scope 3 = kg_HFC134a×GWP_HFC + kg_R404A×GWP_R404 + m³_ww×25 + t_waste×580
Intensity = Total_kgCO₂e ÷ tonnes_output [kgCO₂e/t product]

Netzerra Score Formula

The NTZ Score (0–100) rewards intensity reduction, active offsetting, year-on-year improvement, and engagement. Aligned with SBTi 1.5°C pathway (7% annual reduction).

Intensity_Score = 40 × max(0, 1 − E_intensity / sector_benchmark)
Offset_Score = 30 × min(1, total_offsets / total_emissions)
Reduction_Score = 20 × max(0, ΔE_reduction / 0.07)
Engagement_Score = 10 × min(1, projects_tracked / 10)
NTZ_Score = Σ components [0–100]

The Story Behind Netzerra

Netzerra started as a single question during my industrial attachment at the Kenya Water Industry Association (KWIA) in 2023: "How much carbon does drilling a borehole actually emit?"

I watched drilling rigs burn diesel for days. Steel casings shipped hundreds of kilometres. Cement grout packed into every hole. No one was measuring any of it. I built a small spreadsheet — then a bigger one.

The late Mr. Tom Armstrong, then Chairman of KWIA, became my first mentor. He saw what I was doing and pushed me to think bigger — beyond boreholes, to every sector of Kenya's economy. His passing left a profound gap, and a profound responsibility to see this through.

Three years later, that spreadsheet is Netzerra — covering five sectors, 47 counties, KNCR-native architecture, and the only platform in Kenya that automates Community Development Agreements under the Carbon Markets Regulations 2024. Built by one person, in Kenya, for Kenya.

🚧 MVP Status

Netzerra is a Minimum Viable Product (Version 1.0) currently in active development. Features, data, and calculations are subject to change. The platform has not been audited or certified by any regulatory body.

⚠️ Data Accuracy

Calculations are based on user-inputted data. Netzerra does not independently verify input data. Results must be reviewed by a qualified carbon auditor before submission to NEMA, KNCR, or any VCM standard.

🏢 County Government Data

All county government data, carbon project pipelines, FLLoCA compliance statuses, community benefit figures, and carbon levy revenue figures shown in the County Dashboard are illustrative samples created for demonstration purposes.

Netzerra has no formal partnership, agreement, or data-sharing arrangement with any of the 47 county governments of Kenya, with the Kenya National Carbon Registry (KNCR), with the National Environment Management Authority (NEMA), with the Kenya Water Industry Association (KWIA), with the World Bank Kenya office, or with any bilateral carbon market partner. County names are used to illustrate how the platform would function when formally deployed with real data.

📐 Methodology Disclaimer

Emission factors are sourced from published scientific literature (IPCC 2006, IPCC AR6, IEA 2024, Bath ICE v3.0, World Steel Association 2023, DEFRA/BEIS 2023, KEFRI 2019, GHG Protocol August 2024). These are standard industry references, but:

  • Kenya-specific emission factors may change as KPLC updates its grid mix annually
  • IPCC Tier 1 defaults carry uncertainty ranges of ±5% to ±50% depending on the source
  • Formal KNCR project registration requires Tier 2 or Tier 3 country-specific data
  • The GWP selector defaults to AR6 — confirm with your VVB which standard they require

📱 Payment & Subscriptions

M-Pesa STK Push billing shown in the platform is simulated. No actual payment is processed. Full Safaricom Daraja API integration is on the development roadmap. Membership plan features are indicative of planned functionality.

🔒 Data Storage

Netzerra MVP stores all data in your browser session only. No data is sent to any server. Your calculations, scores, and project lists are cleared when you close the browser tab. Persistent cloud storage with user accounts is on the development roadmap.

⚖️ No Legal or Regulatory Advice

Nothing in this platform constitutes legal, financial, regulatory, or investment advice. Users are responsible for ensuring compliance with Kenya's Climate Change Act 2016 (Amended 2023), the Carbon Markets Regulations 2024, the Carbon Trading Regulations 2025, and all applicable KNCR requirements. Consult qualified legal counsel before making regulatory submissions.
